Our Mission

The New York Historical Times is a digital archive and educational project dedicated to preserving and presenting the journalism of the New York Times from the 1950s — one of the most consequential decades in American history.

We believe that primary sources matter. Reading the news as it was reported — in real time, with the uncertainty and limitations of the moment — gives a depth of understanding that no retrospective account can fully replicate.

A Brief History

The New York Times was founded in 1851 by journalist and politician Henry Jarvis Raymond and former banker George Jones. From its earliest days, it positioned itself as a paper of record — thorough, sober, and committed to factual reporting.

By the 1950s, the Times had established itself as arguably the most influential newspaper in the English-speaking world. Its coverage of the Civil Rights Movement helped bring Southern racial violence to the attention of a national and international audience.

Editorial Standards

The articles presented in this archive are reproduced for educational purposes. Where original text is quoted, it is presented faithfully, including language and framing that reflects the conventions and limitations of 1950s journalism.

Context, background information and explanatory notes have been added by the editorial team of this digital project to assist modern readers in understanding the historical significance of each piece.
