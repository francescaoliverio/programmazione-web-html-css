All about that style

Do you remember those "very usable" and "pretty" websites that we used to navigate during the first years of the internet era?

Well, I do. I remember a time before Flexbox saved our souls. A time when "layout" meant nesting a table inside another table... inside another table.

💣 The "Golden Era" Starter Pack

Back then, we didn't worry about "User Experience" or "Eye Strain." We had priorities. To build a true masterpiece in 1999, you simply had to include:

  • The "Subtle" Background: Usually a tiling GIF of a starry galaxy, a matrix code rain, or a spinning skull on fire. Repeat-x? Repeat-y? Repeat-everything.
  • The Color Palette of Doom: Neon Green text on a Hot Pink background. Contrast ratios specifically designed to give a graphic designer a panic attack.
  • Fonts with Personality: Why use a readable sans-serif when you have Comic Sans, Papyrus, or Courier New?
  • The <marquee> tag: Because why should important text stand still when it can slowly scroll across the screen, making it impossible to read?
  • Under Construction GIFs: A little yellow digger animation to let visitors know you were "working on it" (spoiler: you never finished it).

🏆 From Chaos to Hyperspace

Today, navigating the web feels like a smooth journey through hyperspace. We have CSS Grid, consistent spacing, and dark modes that don't burn our retinas.

But sometimes... don't you miss the chaos? Don't you miss the glitter cursors?
